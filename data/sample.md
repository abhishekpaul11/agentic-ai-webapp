# Shah Rukh Khan (SRK)

> Indian actor, producer, entrepreneur—often called “King Khan.”

**Aliases:** SRK, King Khan, Baadshah of Bollywood  
**Primary domains:** Hindi cinema, entrepreneurship, philanthropy  
**Notable orgs:** Red Chillies Entertainment, Kolkata Knight Riders, Meer Foundation

---

## Fast Facts

- **Born:** 2 Nov 1965, New Delhi, India
- **Education:** St. Columba’s School; Hansraj College (B.A.); Jamia Millia Islamia (Mass Comm., incomplete)
- **Spouse:** Gauri Khan (m. 1991)
- **Children:** Aryan, Suhana, AbRam
- **Companies:** Red Chillies Entertainment (film production/VFX), co-owner of **Kolkata Knight Riders** (IPL)
- **Philanthropy:** **Meer Foundation** (support for acid-attack survivors & women’s empowerment)

---

## One-Paragraph Summary

Shah Rukh Khan is a globally recognized Indian film actor and producer whose career spans television beginnings in the late 1980s to enduring superstardom in Hindi cinema. Known for romantic leads and risk-taking anti-hero roles, he built a diversified portfolio across production, sports ownership, and philanthropy, and remains one of the most commercially successful and culturally influential actors in the world.

---

## Career Timeline (Selected)

- **1989:** TV debut with *Fauji*; gains early recognition
- **1992:** Film debut *Deewana*
- **1993:** Anti-hero turns in *Baazigar* and *Darr*
- **1995:** Breakout romance *Dilwale Dulhania Le Jayenge (DDLJ)*
- **1998–2004:** *Kuch Kuch Hota Hai*, *Devdas*, *Swades*
- **2007:** *Chak De! India* expands image beyond romance
- **2010s:** *My Name Is Khan*, *Chennai Express*, *Raees*
- **2023:** Major comeback with *Pathaan*, *Jawan*, *Dunki*

## Common Q&A (RAG-Ready)

**Q:** Why is Shah Rukh Khan called “King Khan”?  
**A:** It reflects his long-running box-office power, cultural reach, and influence in Bollywood and beyond.

**Q:** What marked his early rise?  
**A:** TV roles (*Fauji*), then bold anti-hero parts in *Baazigar* and *Darr*, followed by *DDLJ* cementing romantic superstardom.

**Q:** What are some socially themed films?  
**A:** *Swades* (rural development/NRI return), *Chak De! India* (women’s sports), *My Name Is Khan* (identity & empathy).

**Q:** What is Meer Foundation’s focus?  
**A:** Supporting women, notably acid-attack survivors, through treatment, legal aid, and rehabilitation.

**Q:** What drove the 2023 resurgence?  
**A:** Back-to-back blockbusters (*Pathaan*, *Jawan*, *Dunki*) spanning action, mass cinema, and social dramedy.
